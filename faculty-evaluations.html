Faculty Evaluations

Supervisor assessments, classroom observations & departmental reviews

Episcopal High School · 2021–2025

Executive Summary

Across four years of formal evaluations, classroom observations, and departmental reviews, supervisors consistently describe exemplary teaching where "one can almost feel the learning happening." Evaluators praise the combination of high expectations with genuine care for each student, clear organization, and warm humor that creates a supportive classroom environment. Beyond teaching, contributions to residential life, advising, and curriculum development demonstrate remarkable flexibility and capability across all aspects of school life.

Comprehensive Faculty Evaluation

Assistant Head for Academics · June 2023
Meets Expectations

"The highlight of the time I got to spend observing and evaluating Hugh Koeze this year was the time that I witnessed him working with a student in his 9th grade English class. The way that Hugh worked to bring out a moment of epiphany for a student who struggled mightily with the fundamentals of reading and writing was truly special. That was the lens through which I was lucky enough to view Hugh this year — a teacher who holds students to high expectations, but who also sees the beautiful uniqueness of each of his students, has a sense of humor, and strives to reach each and every student along the way."

"Hugh's classes were, no surprise, exemplary. This might be the most unscientific sentence ever to be written in an official evaluation, but one can almost feel the learning happening in the classroom. "We are lucky to have Hugh as a central figure for our 9th graders but also for our electives. The variety and depth of his elective offerings add brightness to our curriculum."

I deeply respect that about him. I hope that Hugh will be an important member of the EHS faculty for many years to come."

Observer Notes

  • Students working independently with quiet background music; all engaged
  • Responded to student questions with humor while encouraging independent thinking
  • Seamlessly involved remote student by integrating them into group work
  • Clear, repeated instructions with examples; summarized expectations multiple times
  • Projected student work and facilitated peer feedback
  • Used whiteboard effectively to compare texts and capture ideas
"Hugh is patient, funny, calm and clear. The experience of being in the class feels characterized by respectful humor, light-heartedness, but also commitment to doing serious work... This discussion is so fun!"
